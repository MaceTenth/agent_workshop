How agents think, decompose tasks, and act step by step

Prompt Engineering
The same question produces very different results depending on how you instruct the model. Three key strategies are shown below. Try them side-by-side with any question to see the difference — the classic "How many r's are in strawberry?" is a great start because zero-shot models often get it wrong while chain-of-thought gets it right.
Zero-shot
Just ask
No examples, no reasoning instructions. The model answers from its pre-trained knowledge alone.
System: "You are a helpful assistant. Answer directly."
Few-shot
Show examples first
Prepend 2 worked examples to the prompt so the model learns the expected format and reasoning style.
User: [example 1] → [example 2] → Q: your question
Chain-of-Thought
Think step by step
Tell the model to reason aloud before answering. Often the most reliable for math, counting, and logic.
System: "Think step by step. Show your reasoning, then give your final answer."

Planning & Task Decomposition
Complex tasks are hard to solve in one shot. A planning agent first breaks the goal into smaller, ordered subtasks — then executes them one by one. This mirrors how humans approach difficult problems and dramatically improves reliability.

ReAct: Reason + Act
ReAct (Yao et al., 2022) is a prompting framework where the agent interleaves thinking and acting. Rather than answering in one pass, the model alternates between reasoning about its situation and deciding on an action — then observing the result — until it reaches a final answer. This loop makes agent reasoning transparent and debuggable.
💭 Thought → ⚡ Action → 👁 Observation → 💭 Thought → … ✅ Final Answer
